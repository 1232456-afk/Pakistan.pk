About Pakistan

Officially the Islamic Republic of Pakistan — a sovereign nation in South Asia with a rich history, diverse culture, and strategic importance in the region.

Pakistan is the fifth-most populous country in the world, with a population exceeding 241 million. It has the world's second-largest Muslim population. Islamabad is the nation's capital, while Karachi serves as the largest city and financial hub.

The country covers an area of 881,913 km², making it the 33rd-largest by area. It is bounded by the Arabian Sea to the south, India to the east, Afghanistan and Iran to the west, and China to the northeast. It also shares a maritime border with Oman.

The word Pakistan means "Land of the Pure" in Urdu and Persian — derived from Pak (pure) and Stan (land). The name was coined by Choudhry Rahmat Ali in 1933.

Independence Day — Youm-e-Azadi

Pakistan gained independence on the 27th of Ramadan 1366 AH. India gained independence the following day. Punjab and Bengal were divided along religious lines by the Radcliffe Commission. Pakistan became an Islamic Republic on 23 March 1956 — celebrated as Pakistan Day.

                      Linguistic Diversity

                      Languages of Pakistan

                      Pakistan is home to over 70 languages, with Urdu as the national language, English as official, and rich regional languages spoken by tens of millions.

                      Urdu — قومی زبان

                      Urdu is written in the beautiful Nastaliq calligraphic script — derived from the Persian-Arabic alphabet, written right-to-left. It is the symbol of Pakistani national identity, unifying over 70 linguistic groups.

                      Pakistan has the world's second-largest Urdu-speaking population. Urdu poetry (Shayari), ghazals, and literature are deeply woven into Pakistani culture. The great poet Allama Iqbal composed some of the most celebrated Urdu poetry of all time.
